Overview of Correlations Between Health Risks, Age, and Income


POVERTY VERSUS LACK OF HEALTHCARE, SMOKING and OBESITY:

  • Strong correlation shows increased poverty results in higher rates of people without healthcare.

  • A possible correlations shows a minor increase in smoking for individuals living in higher poverty.

  • Higher degrees of poverty show a possible higher risk of obesity.

AGE VERSUS LACK OF HEALTHCARE, SMOKING and OBESITY:

  • Higher rates of people are covered by healthcare plans in and greater than middle-age.

  • A correlation seems not to exist respective of age versus percentage of popultion that smokes.

  • A correlation doesn't seem evident respective of age versus obesity.

MEDIAN HOUSEHOLD INCOME VERSUS LACK OF HEALTHCARE, SMOKING and OBESITY:

  • A correlation is noticable showing increased household income results in higher rates of people affording healthcare.

  • A correlation indicates that households with lower median income tend to smoke at a higher rate.

  • Similar to smoking percentages, obesity is increased for lower median income levels.
